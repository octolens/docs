Analytics is where you understand what's happening across your keywords over time. Open **Analytics** from the sidebar.

## The three tabs

<Frame caption="Analytics has three tabs at the top of the page.">
  <img src="/images/analytics-overview.png" alt="Analytics page showing General Analytics, AI Summaries, and Conversation Rank tabs" />
</Frame>

- **General Analytics.** Trends and breakdowns across all your tracked keywords. The default tab.
- **AI Summaries.** Weekly AI-generated brief on your brand and competitors. Scale plan only. See [AI Summaries](/learn/ai-summaries).
- **Conversation Rank.** A 0 to 100 score measuring how often your brand appears in Reddit conversations.

## General Analytics

### Page-level filters

These controls apply to every chart on the General Analytics tab:

- **Time range.** Last 7 days (default), Last 30 days, Last 90 days, Last 6 months, Last 1 year, or Custom.
- **Keywords.** Filter to one or more of your tracked keywords. Defaults to All keywords.
- **Only relevant** toggle. **On** by default. Hides low-relevance mentions across every chart so you focus on the conversations that matter.

<Note>
**What Analytics shows by default.** Two scope rules worth knowing:

- **Only relevant mentions are included by default.** Toggle off **Only relevant** in the top right to include low-relevance mentions in every chart.
- **Deleted keywords are not included.** Analytics only shows your currently active keywords. Mentions from keywords you've since deleted won't appear here, even if they were pulled in during the time range you're viewing.
</Note>

<Tip>
Compare time ranges to spot trends. Switch from Last 7 days to Last 90 days to see whether a spike is part of a longer pattern, and use the Keywords picker to isolate one brand or competitor at a time.
</Tip>

### Total mentions

Mentions over time as a line chart by default. Use the chart-level filters to slice by **platform**, **tag**, or **sentiment**.

<Frame caption="Total mentions chart in line view.">
  <img src="/images/analytics-total-mentions.png" alt="Total mentions line chart with platform, tag, and sentiment filters" />
</Frame>

Switch to **bar view** (top right of the chart) to break down volume by keyword, with each keyword shown in its assigned color. A clear way to see which keywords are generating the most conversation.

Click the **download** icon (top right) to export any chart as an image.

### Mentions by Tag

Bar chart showing how mentions break down across AI-assigned tags (Own Brand Mention, Competitor Mention, Industry Insights, Buy Intent, Bug Report, and so on). Useful for understanding the *kind* of conversations happening around your keywords.

See [AI Relevance & Tags](/learn/ai-relevance) for what each tag means.

### Mentions by Platform

A breakdown showing the share of your mentions coming from each platform, with both percentage and raw count. Useful for spotting platforms you should pay more (or less) attention to.

## Conversation Rank

Conversation Rank (CR) is a 0 to 100 score measuring how often your brand appears in Reddit conversations. Think of it as Domain Rating (DR) for the AI age: instead of backlinks, CR tracks how often real people bring up your brand when they discuss tools, compare products, or share advice.

Conversation Rank is **Reddit-only by design**. Reddit is the platform where buying decisions and recommendations get hashed out in real text, which is exactly the signal CR is measuring.

### Score and supporting metrics

The Conversation Rank tab shows:

- **Conversation Rank.** Your brand's score from 0 to 100, calculated for one keyword at a time. Use the keyword picker next to the score to switch.
- **Mentions (last 30 days).** Total Reddit mentions of the keyword in the last 30 days.
- **Unique subreddits (last 30 days).** How many distinct subreddits your brand showed up in. Wider distribution generally means a higher rank.

### Supporting charts

Below the score:

- **Reddit Mentions Over Time.** Line chart of Reddit-only mentions for the selected keyword. Independent time range and keyword controls.
- **Mentions by Subreddit.** Which subreddits your brand is showing up in, ranked by share of mentions.
- **Mentions by Sentiment.** Donut chart showing the Positive / Neutral / Negative split for your Reddit mentions.
