## Keywords

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Where do I edit, pause, or delete a keyword?">
    In the left sidebar, find the keyword in your Keywords list and click the **···** next to its name:

    - **Keyword settings.** Edit type, platforms, context, and include/exclude rules.
    - **Pause keyword.** Stop pulling new mentions without deleting; existing mentions stay in your feed.
    - **Delete keyword.** Permanently remove the keyword and all its mentions. Can't be undone.

    See [Choose your keywords](/quickstart/keywords) for more.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does a keyword match partial words?">
    By default, no. **Exact match is on** for new keywords, so Octolens won't match `Octolensapi`. However, we will catch your keyword within a domain, with `'s`, or in a handle (e.g. `@octolens`).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I rename my workspace without losing keywords or mentions?">
    If you want to change the business context for which you're tracking keywords, go to **Settings → Company Context** and update the answers in the form. We won't backfill that data, but from then on all incoming posts will use the updated information for AI relevance and tagging.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I track all posts from a specific subreddit (e.g. all posts in r/yourcompany)?">
    Yes. Instead of pasting in a keyword, paste in the URL of the subreddit into the keyword input and click save. Then we'll know to track all posts of the subreddit.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Platforms

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What platforms do you track?">
    [Here are all the platforms we track.](/learn/platforms/overview) Depending on your plan, you'll have access to all or a subset of them.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why am I not seeing mentions from a specific platform?">
    A few common reasons, in roughly the order to check:

    1. **Backfill window.** Octolens looks back 7 days when you add a new keyword. Older posts won't appear.
    2. **Refresh interval hasn't run yet.** Each platform has its own refresh interval. See [Tracked Platforms](/learn/platforms/overview) for specifics.
    3. **Platform is disabled for this keyword.** Check the Platforms field in [Keyword Settings](/quickstart/keyword-settings).
    4. **Coverage varies by platform.** We get close to 100% coverage on X/Twitter, Reddit, YouTube, and a few others. For LinkedIn, we fetch all tagged brand mentions via the OAuth flow, and for other keywords we find what a logged-out user would see.

    If you've checked all of the above and still don't see mentions, reach out via in-app chat with the post URL and we'll investigate.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How often is each platform refreshed?">
    Refresh intervals vary by platform and plan. Reddit, Bluesky, and Podcasts are real-time. Otherwise the Pro plan is hourly across most platforms; the Scale plan is real-time on most platforms. See [Tracked Platforms](/learn/platforms/overview) for the exact interval per platform.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why is LinkedIn returning so few mentions?">
    LinkedIn is the most restricted platform we monitor. Two things that help:

    - **Connect a LinkedIn page admin.** Go to **Settings → Platforms** to authorize a LinkedIn account. Once connected, you'll get all tagged company mentions.
    - **Set realistic expectations for non-brand keywords.** For these we find what a logged-out user can see, so we may miss posts you'd otherwise see in LinkedIn.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I connect LinkedIn for tagged company mentions?">
    Go to **Settings → Platforms**, find LinkedIn, and click **Connect**. You'll authorize Octolens to read your LinkedIn page activity. Once connected, mentions tagging your company appear in your feed.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I increase a platform refresh frequency?">
    If you need a faster refresh than our Scale plan offers for a platform, reach out via chat or [hi@octolens.com](mailto:hi@octolens.com) for an enterprise plan. These begin at $1,000 per month billed annually.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why didn't this specific post show up in my feed?">
    A few reasons we may not catch a specific post:

    - The post is older than our 7-day backfill window
    - The keyword is paused or the platform is disabled in [Keyword Settings](/quickstart/keyword-settings)
    - The post uses your keyword in a way our parser misses (a partial match with Exact match on, or a non-text format like an embedded image)

    Send us the post URL via in-app chat and we'll check.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do you have multi-language support?">
    The app UI is in English, but you can search for any word in any language. On Twitter/X, we only return English results. Make sure you don't have "EN only" selected in your feed so other languages aren't hidden.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Alerts and notifications

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do you have a Slack integration?">
    Yes. You can get alerts for new keyword mentions in Slack. See [Connect Slack](/quickstart/connect-slack) and [set up alerts](/quickstart/alerts).

    You can decide which relevance levels we should include. For example, you can get alerted for only posts our AI deems as High relevance (e.g. with buy-intent, great engagement opportunities, customer complaints or praise).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can a single alert send to multiple destinations?">
    Yes. In the Add alert dialog (Step 2), you can toggle on Email, Slack, and Webhook all on the same alert, and each fires off the same view.

    Each destination has its own frequency (Instantly, Hourly, Daily, Weekly).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why isn't my Slack alert posting?">
    Most often it's one of three things:

    1. The Slack channel is private and Octolens doesn't have access.
    2. The view filter isn't matching anything. Open the view in your feed and check that mentions appear there. If the view is empty, the alert has nothing to send.
    3. The Slack integration disconnected. Go to **Settings → Integration for Slack** and check the status. If it's not Active, remove and reconnect.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why isn't the Slack channel I just created showing up in Octolens?">
    Octolens caches your Slack channel list when you connect. Newly created channels sometimes don't appear until you reconnect.

    To refresh: **Settings → Integration for Slack** → click **Remove integration for Slack**, then connect again. The new channel will now show up in alert setup.

    The channel also needs to be a public channel that you're a part of.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="A keyword isn't returning any mentions, what should I check?">
    Run through this checklist:

    1. **Wait at least an hour after adding the keyword.** Initial backfill and platform refresh take time.
    2. **Is it too narrow?** Check out [Keyword Settings](/quickstart/keyword-settings). Your keyword may be too narrow, long-tail, or have too many settings to be finding any results.
    3. **Are all platforms enabled?** Check the Platforms field in Keyword Settings. By default they all should be, but it's worth confirming.
    4. **Check All mentions, not just For you.** Your filter or default view might be hiding the post. **All mentions** shows everything Octolens has pulled in, including low-relevance.
    5. **Is the keyword paused?** A paused keyword stops pulling new mentions. Resume it from the **···** menu.
    6. **Is your company context strong?** Thin context leads to over-aggressive AI filtering. See [Company Context](/quickstart/company-context).

    If you've checked all of the above and still see nothing, reach out via in-app chat with the keyword and an example post URL we should have caught.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why are some mentions tagged Low relevance?">
    Octolens uses AI to score every mention as relevant or not relevant based on your company context, the post content, and the source. Low-relevance mentions are ones where the AI isn't confident the post is actually about your product or industry. For example, a generic mention of a common-word brand, or a post in a language or context unrelated to your business.

    You can:

    - See low-relevance mentions in the **All mentions** view
    - Filter them out of any custom view or alert
    - Help the AI learn by marking mentions as Relevant or Not Relevant on individual cards
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I update a wrongly tagged or scored mention?">
    On any mention card, click **Mark as not relevant** or **Mark as relevant** to give feedback.

    You can also adjust the keyword itself. Adding **Negative terms**, **Negative authors**, or sharper **Keyword context** in [Keyword Settings](/quickstart/keyword-settings) usually has a bigger impact than per-mention feedback.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why are my mentions used up? I see less mentions than my monthly quota in my feed and analytics.">
    Both relevant and irrelevant mentions count against your monthly mention limit. Every post that matches your keyword is counted, regardless of whether the AI marks it as relevant or irrelevant. This is why it's important to set up your keywords carefully. Broad or noisy keywords will use up your quota faster.

    To see how many mentions are being found, go to the **All mentions** view or go to [Analytics](/learn/analytics) and toggle off "only relevant."
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
