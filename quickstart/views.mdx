title: Views

Views are saved filters over your mentions feed. Think of them as playlists for what you want to pay attention to. Most customers live inside a handful of views rather than scrolling through every mention.

Every view can also become an [alert](/quickstart/alerts), so mentions matching that view get sent to Slack, email, or a webhook.

## How the Views sidebar is laid out

<Frame caption="The Views sidebar, with default views on top and your custom views below.">
  <img src="/images/views-sidebar.png" alt="Octolens Views sidebar showing For you, All mentions, and default and custom views" />
</Frame>

Two views sit above the **Views** header and are always pinned:

- **For you:** a curated mix of your Own Brand, Competitor, and Industry Term keyword mentions. A good default home screen.
- **All mentions:** literally every mention Octolens pulls in, **including low-relevance ones**. Use this when you're investigating what's being filtered out.

Below the Views header sit the **default views** (pre-configured for common workflows) and any **custom views** you've created.

## Default views

| View | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **For you** | A mix of mentions tagged Own Brand Mention, Competitor Mention, and Industry Insights |
| **All mentions** | Every mention, both relevant and not relevant |
| **Brand Monitoring** | Posts that mention your brand directly, tag `Own Brand Mention` |
| **Competitor Intelligence** | Posts that mention your competitors. Useful for market sentiment, content ideas, and gap-spotting |
| **Buy Intent** | Posts where people express intent to buy or switch tools, like "looking for a \[tool]" or "alternatives to \[competitor]" |
| **Industry insights** | Posts tagged `Industry Insights`, conversations about your category whether or not they mention your brand |
| **Crisis Management** | Posts with negative sentiment about your brand. Use this to act quickly and protect your reputation |

You can edit any default view. Just open it, adjust the filters, and save.

## Feed controls

Every view shows the same controls at the top:

- **Time range:** Last 7 days (default), Last 30 days, Last 90 days, Last 6 months, Last 1 year, or Custom
- **Only unread** toggle: hide mentions you've already seen
- **Relevant vs. total counter:** e.g. *"Showing 13 relevant mentions out of 1,494 total mentions."* Tells you how tight your filters are.

## Custom views

Build your own view when none of the defaults match your workflow.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click + Add view">
    Next to the **Views** header in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add filters">
    Click **+ Add filter** and pick one or more of the categories below. Each filter narrows your results.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the view">
    Give it a name and save. It appears in your sidebar under Views.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You can also open any existing view, tweak the filters, and save changes, or duplicate it under a new name.

## Available filters

<Frame caption="The Add filter menu. Each filter narrows results; combine them freely.">
  <img src="/images/views-add-filter.png" alt="Add filter dropdown showing Keyword, Platform, Language, Tag, Sentiment, X Followers, and Advanced filter options" />
</Frame>

### Keyword

Scope to one or more of your tracked keywords. Example: narrow a view to just `Octolens` + `social listening`.

### Platform

Filter by source. Options: LinkedIn, Stack Overflow, GitHub, DEV, Reddit, Hacker News, Twitter/X, YouTube, TikTok, Bluesky, Newsletter. See [Tracked Platforms](/learn/platforms/overview) for what's monitored where.

### Language

Filter by detected language. Supported: English, Spanish, French, Chinese, Portuguese, Russian, Italian, Japanese, Korean, German, Dutch.

### Tag

Filter by AI-assigned tag. Every mention gets one or more of these:

`Own Brand Mention` · `Competitor Mention` · `Industry Insights` · `Product Question` · `Buy Intent` · `User Feedback` · `Bug Report` · `Hiring` · `Event` · `Promotional Post` · `AI generated`

See [AI Relevance & Tags](/learn/ai-relevance) for what each tag means.

### Sentiment

Filter by `Positive`, `Neutral`, or `Negative`. See [Sentiment Analysis](/learn/sentiment).

### X Followers

On X/Twitter mentions only, filter by the author's follower count. Set a **Min** and/or **Max**. Useful for "only show posts from influencers" (min 10,000) or "only show posts from everyday users" (max 5,000).

## Advanced filter

When simple AND-across-filters isn't enough, use Advanced filter to build nested Boolean logic.

<Frame caption="The Advanced filter builder. Combine rules and groups to express complex logic.">
  <img src="/images/views-advanced-filter.png" alt="Advanced filter UI with a Where clause, filter/operator/value dropdowns, and options to add a single filter rule, a filter group, or delete the advanced filter" />
</Frame>

Each rule has three parts:

- **Field:** pick a filter like Tag, Sentiment, Keyword, Platform, Language, or X Followers
- **Operator:** for example `Contains`, or a comparator for numeric fields
- **Values:** one or more values for that field

From there, you can:

- **+ Add single filter rule:** add another rule at the same level, combined with AND
- **+ Add filter group:** nest a set of rules inside parentheses, giving you AND/OR composition (e.g. *(A AND B) OR C*)
- **Delete advanced filter:** remove the advanced filter entirely

> **Example.** *(Sentiment = Negative AND Tag = Own Brand Mention) OR Tag = Bug Report*. A view that surfaces anything negative about your brand, plus any bug reports regardless of sentiment.

Advanced filter appears as its own pill alongside regular filters at the top of the view, so you can still layer standard filters (like language) on top of it.

## Edit or delete a view

- **Edit a view:** open it, change the filters or time range, and save.
- **Delete a view:** click the **···** next to the view name in the sidebar and choose **Delete**. Default views can be edited but not deleted.

## Turn a view into an alert

Every view, default or custom, can be wired up to send new matching mentions to Slack, email, or a webhook.

See [Alerts](/quickstart/alerts) for setup.
