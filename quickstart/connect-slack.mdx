Connecting Slack is a one-time setup per workspace. Once it's done, every [alert](/quickstart/alerts) you create can be sent to one or more Slack channels.

This page covers the **workspace connection** only. You pick which channels receive a given alert — and how often — when you create the alert itself.

## Connect your Slack workspace

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings">
    Open **Settings** in your Octolens workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Integration for Slack tab">
    Click the **Integration for Slack** tab at the top of the Settings page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize Octolens in Slack">
    Click **Connect** and authorize the Octolens app for your Slack workspace. You'll be redirected to Slack to approve the permissions, then bounced back to Octolens.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Confirm you're connected

After authorizing, the Integration for Slack page shows:

- **Workspace** — the name of your connected Slack workspace
- **Status** — `Active` when the connection is healthy

If you see this, you're done. Slack will now appear as a destination option when you create or edit an alert.

## What you can send to Slack

Any view-based alert can be sent to Slack, which means you control exactly which mentions reach your team. Some common patterns:

- Send **Brand Monitoring** mentions to a dedicated `#brand-watch` channel
- Send **Buy Intent** posts to your sales team's channel
- Send **Crisis Management** alerts to a leadership or comms channel
- Send a custom view (e.g. "negative mentions of our pricing") to the product team

See [Views](/quickstart/views) for the default views and how to build custom ones.

## Pick channels and frequency

Channel selection and delivery frequency are configured **per alert**, not on this page:

- **Slack channels** — multi-select; an alert can post to more than one channel
- **Frequency** — Instantly, Hourly, Daily, or Weekly (batched)

See [Alerts](/quickstart/alerts) for the full alert setup flow.

## Disconnect or switch workspaces

To disconnect Slack, click **Remove integration for Slack** on the Integration for Slack tab.

To switch to a different Slack workspace, remove the existing integration and then connect again — you'll be able to pick a new workspace during authorization.

<Note>
Removing the integration stops all Slack deliveries immediately. Alerts configured to send to Slack will pause until a new workspace is connected.
</Note>
